Course Description:

Further topics in regression and ANOVA; non-parametric methods; model selection and verification; use of statistical software; additional selected topics.

Prerequisites:

STAT 3011 or 3021 or equivalent. (This is a very important requirement. Talk to the instructor if you didn't take any introductory statistics course before.)

Mathematics knowledge: algebra only, no calculus needed to take this course.

Textbook:

Officially, the textbook is Fred Ramsey and Dan Schafer (2012) The Statistical Sleuth (3rd Edition)

But second edition is fine: Fred Ramsey and Dan Schafer (2002) The Statistical Sleuth (2nd Edition).

The homework problems are from the 3rd edtion textbook. In case some students still use the old one, each time scanned homework problems will be posted on moodle site.

The difference of these two editions is not significant, and new materials in 3rd edition will NOT be covered in our course.

Homework:

Homework will be assigned on a weekly basis and it is due on Wednesday class.

Incompletes:

University and department policy is that "I" grades are used only when there is a small amount of unfinished work that the student can complete on his or her own before the end of the following semester, when there was a legitimate excuse why the work could not be done on time, and when arrangements have been made with the instructor as to when the work will be done.
